Partner Portfolio — Coverage & Segmentation

How many partners we have, how they split by segment, turnover (GMV) and profitability, how much the team currently covers — and why there is no capacity to take on new partners, especially in SMB and Mid-market (MM).

Executive summary (30 sec)

  • Portfolio: 488 partners / 4 328 locations / €1 346 150 GMV over Jan–May 2026. Steep pyramid: Enterprise + MM = 10.7% of partners but 84.7% of GMV.
  • SMB is a long tail: 432 partners (88.5%) generate just 15.3% of GMV. Average SMB partner = €475 GMV vs €37 165 for Enterprise — a ~78× gap.
  • The team actively manages 38 key partners with only 3 account managers (AM) — covering the large majority of portfolio GMV.
  • Load is critically skewed: one AM (M. Brynchak) carries 1 626 locations and ~75.9% of portfolio GMV. Meanwhile 1 484 locations across 204 partners have no AM assigned.
  • Conclusion: there is no capacity to onboard more managed partners. Every new SMB/MM partner consumes the same effort as Enterprise but returns a fraction of the value.

How to read it

  • For the same managing effort, an Enterprise partner delivers €37 165 of GMV; an SMB partner delivers only €475.
  • SMB is 88.5% of partners but just 15.3% of GMV. As a tail it can only be served at scale (self-serve / automation), not one-by-one by an AM.
  • Note on profitability: SMB runs a positive CP L1 (€25 741) on high commission rates, while Enterprise/MM are intentionally subsidised (campaigns & incentives) and run negative CP L1 to drive volume. So the constraint is not SMB profitability per euro — it is that each SMB partner is simply too small to justify hands-on management.

7. Conclusion: why there is no capacity for new partners

Argument

  • 1. Only 3 AMs for the whole key portfolio. They already cover 3 156 locations (31 partners + 7 central chains). The team is physically at its limit.
  • 2. Concentration risk. One AM (M. Brynchak) holds ~75.9% of GMV and 1 626 locations. Adding partners without adding people deepens the imbalance.
  • 3. Economics against SMB/MM. A new SMB partner brings on average just €475 of GMV yet needs the same onboarding and ongoing support as an Enterprise partner at €37 165 — a ~78× worse effort-to-value ratio.
  • 4. Focus on value. 1 484 locations across 204 partners already have no AM. We are not even fully covering the existing book; spreading thinner onto small SMB/MM would weaken work with top partners.
  • 5. The tail does not scale by hand. 432 SMB partners cannot be managed individually with the current team — that is a self-serve / automation job, not an AM job.

Recommended instead of "take more"

  • Freeze intake of new SMB/MM into hands-on management until additional headcount is added.
  • First cover (or move to self-serve) the 1 484 locations that currently have no AM.
  • Rebalance the book to remove the ~75.9% GMV single-AM concentration risk.
  • Onboard new partners only selectively in Enterprise / high-MM above segment-average GMV.
  • Serve the SMB tail through automated / group processes, not individual management.
